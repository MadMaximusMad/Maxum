# Market Layer

**Markets are the environments in which deployed capital becomes active.**

Liquidity is integrated into trading environments where it enables exchange and price discovery. These environments form the interface between capital and activity.

Without markets, capital remains inactive. With markets, liquidity becomes productive infrastructure capable of generating measurable output.

The structure and distribution of liquidity across markets determine the system’s ability to sustain activity and generate revenue.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Interpretation**

Markets are not external to the system. They are the environments in which deployed capital becomes active and measurable.
{% endhint %}
