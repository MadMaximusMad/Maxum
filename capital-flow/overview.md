# Overview

Capital flow defines how value moves through the Maxum system.

Maxum operates as a closed capital system in which capital is formed, deployed, activated through market usage, converted into revenue, and consolidated back into the balance sheet. This flow is not incidental to the system. It is the system’s operating logic.

Rather than treating liquidity, activity, and treasury growth as separate functions, Maxum links them into a single process. Capital enters through fees and direct capital intake, is deployed into liquidity, generates market activity, and returns to the balance sheet as usable system capital. As this process repeats, the system increases its capacity to support deeper markets, greater throughput, and further capital formation.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Key framing**\
Capital flow should be understood as the continuous movement of capital through formation, deployment, activity, revenue, and balance sheet reinforcement, rather than as a one-time transfer into the system.
{% endhint %}
