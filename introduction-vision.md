# Vision

**Maxum exists to transform liquidity into a continuously compounding capital system.**

On-chain economies require liquidity to function, yet most systems treat liquidity as transient and externally incentivized. Under these conditions, capital remains unstable and market depth deteriorates as incentives decline.

Maxum replaces this model with a system in which liquidity, capital, and activity are structurally interdependent. Liquidity is deployed capital, capital is generated from activity, and activity is enabled by liquidity. These relationships form a continuous cycle:

> Activity → Revenue → Capital → Liquidity → Activity

The objective is not to maximize participation through incentives, but to maximize the efficiency of capital as it moves through this loop. Usage strengthens the system, the system expands its own capacity, and growth emerges from sustained throughput rather than external input.

{% hint style="success" %}
**System relationship**

Usage strengthens the system that enables usage.
{% endhint %}

Maxum therefore functions as a financial infrastructure layer for on-chain economies, where liquidity is continuously produced, utilized, and compounded.

The operating form of that vision is defined in [System Loop](system/system-loop.md), while the movement of capital through the system is described in [Capital Formation](capital-flow/README.md) and [Capital Deployment](capital-flow/capital-deployment.md).
