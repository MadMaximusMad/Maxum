# \# Liquidity \& Markets

# 

# \## Liquidity Layer

# 

# The liquidity layer represents all capital actively deployed across markets within the system.

# 

# In Maxum, liquidity is not static. It is continuously deployed, adjusted, and expanded based on system conditions. This allows the system to support markets where activity exists while reinforcing areas that require additional depth.

# 

# By treating liquidity as an active component rather than a passive pool, Maxum ensures that markets remain functional and efficient over time. Liquidity is always positioned to support interaction, rather than waiting to be utilized.

# 

# \## Market Structure

# 

# Markets within Maxum are formed through MAX trading pairs, where assets are paired against MAX to enable exchange.

# 

# Pricing emerges through automated market mechanisms in which supply, demand, and liquidity depth interact. As liquidity increases, markets become more stable and price discovery improves. Users are able to transact with lower slippage and more predictable outcomes.

# 

# Over time, this creates a more efficient market environment where assets can be exchanged reliably and where pricing reflects sustained activity rather than temporary distortions.

# 

# \## Liquidity Under Management (LUM)

# 

# Liquidity Under Management represents the total capital actively deployed across all markets.

# 

# From a structural perspective, LUM reflects the system’s capacity to support trading activity. It aggregates all liquidity positions and deployed capital, providing a measure of how much market depth the system can sustain.

# 

# As LUM increases, markets benefit from deeper liquidity, reduced price impact, and improved execution. Growth in LUM is driven by revenue accumulation, capital formation, and expansion into new markets.

# 

# This makes LUM one of the most important indicators of system strength, as it directly influences how effectively markets can operate.

# 

# \## Capital Allocation Strategy

# 

# Capital within Maxum is deployed based on where it can generate the greatest impact.

# 

# Allocation decisions take into account market demand, application requirements, liquidity efficiency, and growth potential. Rather than distributing capital evenly, the system directs it toward areas where it can improve market conditions and support activity.

# 

# This ensures that liquidity is productive. Capital is continuously repositioned to support the parts of the system that require it most, allowing Maxum to maintain efficiency as it scales.
