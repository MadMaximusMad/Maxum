# Supply Model

**Supply is dynamically constrained by balance sheet capital.**

The MAX asset operates under an elastic supply model governed by the system’s balance sheet. Circulating supply cannot exceed the Risk-Free Value held within the balance sheet. This constraint enforces a direct relationship between capital and supply.

> Circulating Supply ≤ Balance Sheet RFV

Supply expansion occurs only when additional capital is accumulated. As capital enters the system through fee generation or deposits, RFV increases, enabling controlled expansion of supply within the constraint.

Conversely, when system conditions require contraction, supply is reduced through burn mechanisms and capital deployment strategies. This ensures that circulating supply remains aligned with underlying capital at all times.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Constraint**

Supply cannot expand independently of balance sheet capital.
{% endhint %}

Supply is therefore not an independent growth vector. It is a derivative of capital state.

This constraint depends on [Balance Sheet](../capital-flow/balance-sheet.md) and is adjusted in [Capital Expansion & Contraction](capital-expansion-and-contraction.md).
