# Capital Expansion & Contraction

**The system regulates supply relative to balance sheet state.**

When market conditions reflect excess demand relative to balance sheet backing, the system allows capital inflows. These inflows increase RFV and enable controlled supply expansion within defined constraints.

When market conditions reverse and supply exceeds efficient levels relative to capital, the system deploys balance sheet capital to reduce circulating supply.

This process ensures that supply remains structurally aligned with capital, without requiring discretionary intervention.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**Interpretation**

The objective is not price targeting. The objective is maintaining alignment between supply and capital.
{% endhint %}

Expansion increases capital capacity. Contraction restores balance between circulating supply and underlying capital.
