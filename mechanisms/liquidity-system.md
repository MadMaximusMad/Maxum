# Liquidity System

**Liquidity is deployed capital that produces system revenue.**

Liquidity within Maxum is not externally sourced. It is derived from balance sheet capital and deployed into markets where it can generate activity.

Liquidity providers participate in system-level revenue through a proportional distribution model. Rewards are determined by the relationship between individual liquidity share and total system throughput.

As activity increases, fee generation increases. As fee generation increases, distributions to liquidity providers increase. This reinforces the presence of liquidity within markets and sustains system throughput.

{% hint style="success" %}
**Reinforcing structure**

Liquidity enables activity, activity generates fees, and fees sustain liquidity.
{% endhint %}

Liquidity is therefore both an input and an output of the system.

Liquidity is funded by [Capital Deployment](../capital-flow/capital-deployment.md), monetized through [Activity Layer](../capital-flow/activity-layer.md), and rewarded through [Fee System](fee-system.md).
