# Overview

Mechanisms define the rules that convert capital state into system behavior.

The Maxum system is governed by a set of linked mechanisms that regulate supply, fee capture, liquidity formation, capital expansion and contraction, and staking efficiency. These mechanisms do not operate independently. Each exists to preserve the relationship between balance sheet capital, circulating supply, and market liquidity.

Supply expands only when supported by Risk-Free Value in the balance sheet. Fees convert transaction activity into burns, liquidity reinforcement, and treasury growth. Liquidity is system-controlled rather than externally rented. Expansion and contraction mechanisms regulate how capital enters and exits circulation, while staking structures align participant behavior with long-term capital efficiency.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Key framing**\
Mechanisms are not user-facing features layered on top of the system. They are the execution logic through which Maxum maintains capital discipline, captures activity, and redeploys value.
{% endhint %}
