# \# Operational Mechanics

# 

# \## Staking

# 

# Staking allows users to participate in system growth by committing MAX for a defined period.

# 

# These commitments reduce circulating supply and align participants with the long-term development of the system. Rewards are distributed over time, creating a predictable mechanism that avoids sudden changes in supply or distribution.

# 

# Because staking encourages longer-term participation, it contributes to system stability while allowing participants to benefit from ongoing growth.

# 

# \## Bonds

# 

# Bonds enables users to contribute assets directly to the system.

# 

# In return, they receive MAX at a discounted rate, with tokens vesting over time. This ensures that supply enters circulation gradually rather than all at once.

# 

# This mechanism strengthens the capital base while maintaining control over supply expansion. It aligns the introduction of new supply with the growth of underlying assets, ensuring that system expansion is supported by real value.

# 

# \## Liquidity Participation

# 

# Users can provide liquidity to markets and participate in the revenue generated by system activity.

# 

# By contributing to market depth, liquidity participants enable trading and interaction across the system. In return, they receive a share of the fees generated by that activity.

# 

# Rewards are distributed based on participation, ensuring that those who support the system’s liquidity layer are compensated in proportion to their contribution.

# 

# \## Referral System

# 

# The referral system supports organic growth by rewarding users who bring new participants into the system.

# 

# Incentives are tied to activity, ensuring that growth contributes to the system’s expansion rather than short-term speculation. This aligns user behavior with long-term system development and encourages sustainable participation.
