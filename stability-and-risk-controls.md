# Stability & Risk Controls

## Capital Stability Mechanisms

Maxum incorporates mechanisms designed to maintain balance within the system as conditions change.

When demand is strong, supply can expand in a controlled manner through capital formation. When market conditions weaken, the system can deploy capital to buy back and remove MAX from circulation.

This dynamic allows the system to respond to both growth and contraction while maintaining structural stability.

## Market Protection Systems

Market protections are designed to reduce the impact of manipulation and short-term volatility.

Time-weighted average pricing mechanisms ensure that system behavior is based on stable signals rather than temporary price distortions. This helps prevent manipulation and ensures that system decisions reflect sustained market conditions.

These protections contribute to a more reliable and predictable market environment.

## Withdrawal Controls

During periods of extreme volatility, withdrawal pacing mechanisms can be implemented.

These controls are designed to prevent rapid capital outflows that could destabilize markets. By moderating the speed of withdrawals, the system is given time to adjust and maintain equilibrium.

This helps protect both the system and its participants during periods of stress.
