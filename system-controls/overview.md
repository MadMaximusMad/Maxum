# Overview

System controls preserve structural integrity under changing market conditions.

Maxum does not rely on discretionary intervention to maintain coherence. Instead, it uses explicit control systems to protect the relationship between capital, supply, liquidity, and market behavior. These controls govern stability, constrain risk, and protect the infrastructure through which the capital loop operates.

This includes mechanisms that limit unstable expansion, support contraction when necessary, resist manipulation, and secure treasury and contract execution. The purpose of system controls is not to eliminate volatility entirely, but to ensure that volatility does not break the balance sheet logic or disrupt the system’s ability to retain and redeploy capital.

{% hint style="info" %}
**Key framing**\
System controls should be understood as structural protections that preserve continuity of the capital loop, rather than as reactive tools used only during stress.
{% endhint %}
