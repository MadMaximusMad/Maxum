# Risk Controls

**Risk controls prevent localized disruptions from propagating through the system.**

The system is exposed to variability in activity, liquidity distribution, and participant behavior. These risks are managed through structural design rather than external intervention.

Liquidity is distributed across markets to reduce dependency on any single venue. Continuous fee accumulation ensures that capital grows as long as activity persists, creating an expanding buffer within the balance sheet.

Withdrawal constraints introduce time-based friction during periods of rapid decline, preventing immediate depletion of deployed liquidity.

{% hint style="warning" %}
**System property**

Risk is not eliminated. It is absorbed and distributed through system structure.
{% endhint %}
