# Tokenomics

## Overview

MAX is the central asset within the Maxum system. It functions as the core unit through which value flows across markets, participation mechanisms, and application integrations.

Its role extends beyond simple ownership. MAX is embedded within the system’s structure, acting as both a liquidity asset and a coordination layer between different parts of the ecosystem. As liquidity expands and usage increases, MAX becomes more integral to how value moves within the system.

## Value Accrual

The value of MAX is driven by the growth of the system itself.

As liquidity is deployed and markets generate activity, fees are produced and captured as revenue. This revenue strengthens the capital base, enabling further liquidity deployment. As this process repeats, the system expands its capacity and deepens its markets.

At the same time, MAX becomes increasingly utilized across trading pairs and applications. As usage increases, its role within the system strengthens, and its importance as a market asset grows.

Value accrual is therefore not isolated to a single mechanism. It emerges from the interaction between liquidity, activity, and revenue.

## Supply Model

MAX operates under an elastic supply model, allowing supply to adjust in response to system conditions.

Expansion occurs when the system grows and requires additional liquidity or capital alignment. Contraction occurs through mechanisms such as burn, which reduce circulating supply over time.

This model ensures that supply remains aligned with the system’s underlying capital base. By maintaining this relationship, the system avoids uncontrolled expansion and preserves structural integrity.

The constraint that circulating supply remains tied to Risk-Free Value provides a foundation for sustainable growth, ensuring that supply is supported by real assets within the system.

## MAX as Base Asset

MAX functions as a base asset through its role in liquidity and market structure.

As more assets are paired against MAX, it becomes the asset through which markets are accessed and value is exchanged. Users increasingly interact with MAX when trading, and applications integrate it as a core component of their market systems.

Over time, this leads to liquidity concentration around MAX. As liquidity deepens, it becomes the most efficient route between assets, reinforcing its role within the system.

This process is driven by usage. MAX becomes central not because it is designated as such, but because it is consistently used across markets.
